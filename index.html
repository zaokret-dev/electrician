Panel upgrade costs vary based on the current panel size, desired amperage, and the age of your home. A typical 100A to 200A upgrade in Chicago ranges from $1,800 to $3,500, including permits and inspection. We always provide a detailed written estimate before any work begins — no surprise charges.
Yes, always. Permit-required work performed without a permit can void your homeowner's insurance, create problems when selling your home, and — most importantly — is genuinely dangerous. We pull all required permits through the City of Chicago and handle scheduling the inspection. We do not cut corners.
Yes. We install Level 2 (240V) EV charging stations for all major vehicle brands including Tesla, Ford, GM, Rivian, and others. We assess your panel capacity, run the dedicated circuit, install the charger, and handle permits. Most installs are completed in a single day.
Knob-and-tube wiring (common in Chicago homes built before 1950) is not inherently dangerous if it's in good condition and unmodified, but it's ungrounded and cannot support modern electrical loads. Most insurance companies require it to be replaced, and it does not support GFCI protection. We recommend a full assessment and typically advise full rewiring for homes with active knob-and-tube systems.
